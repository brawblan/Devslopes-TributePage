Alan Abel

1928-2020

Musician. Educator. Inventor. Inspiration.
Alan Abel (December 6, 1928 â€“ April 25, 2020) was an American percussionist, music educator, and inventor of musical instruments. He was the associate principal percussionist of the Philadelphia Orchestra from 1959 until his retirement in 1997. He is widely regarded as one of the most important percussion educators of the second half of the twentieth century, having taught at Temple University beginning in 1972.[1] Abel's inventions include several unique and ubiquitous triangles and a bass drum stand that allowed the instrument to be suspended with the use of rubber bands.

Timeline

  • 6 Dec 1928- born in Hobart, Indiana
  • 1935- started percussion lessons at the age of 7
  • 1947 to 1951- studied at the Eastman School of Music with Haskell Harr and William Street
  • 1951 to 1953- enlisted and played in the United States Air Force Band
  • 1953 to 1959- performed with the Oklahoma City Philharmonic
  • 1959 to 1997- joined the Philadelphia Orchestra and remained there until the end of his career
  • 1963- manufacturing began of the famous "Alan Abel Triangle"
  • 1998- inducted into the Percussive Arts Society Hall of Fame
  • 2012- Awarded honorary Doctorate of Music by the New England Conservatory of Music
  • 25 April 2020- passed away of complications from COVID-19
